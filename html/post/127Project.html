Detection of Adversarial Attacks in a Cyclic Power Network


This project involved designing and implementing a simulation-based approach to detect adversarial attacks in a cyclic power network. By leveraging Lasso regression with sparsity-inducing regularization, we achieved a 99% success rate in detecting and isolating attack locations under noisy and adversarial conditions.

Objectives

  • Primary Objective: Detect all attacked nodes and lines in the network with a high success rate.
  • Secondary Objectives:
    • Simulate a realistic cyclic power network with adversarial conditions.
    • Evaluate the empirical performance of Lasso regression in detecting attack locations.
    • Demonstrate the practical use of sparsity-inducing regularization for solving real-world problems.

Methodology

  • Network Simulation:
    • Constructed a cyclic power network with 100 nodes and edges.
    • Randomly generated network parameters:
      • Line reactances uniformly sampled from [0.1, 0.2].
      • Nodal phases uniformly sampled from [−π/18, π/18].
  • Measurement Generation: Calculated true power flows and nodal powers based on network physics.
  • Attack Simulation:
    • Simulated attacks on 10 randomly selected nodes and 10 specific edges.
    • Corrupted sensors with adversarial noise in addition to Gaussian noise.
  • Lasso Regression:
    • Formulated the problem as a Lasso optimization using CVXPY.
    • Enforced sparsity to isolate attacked locations.
  • Performance Evaluation:
    • Declared success if:
      • Non-attacked nodes/edges satisfied |v| ≤ 0.01.
      • Attacked nodes/edges satisfied |v| > 0.01.
    • Repeated the experiment 100 times to calculate the empirical success rate.

Results

  • Empirical Success Rate: Achieved 99% accuracy in identifying attack locations.
  • Demonstrated the robustness of Lasso regression under various adversarial conditions.

Skills Applied

  • Optimization and Modeling: Formulated sparse regression problems with ℓ1-regularization to enforce sparsity.
  • Programming: Used Python, NumPy, and CVXPY for simulations and optimization workflows.
  • Simulation and Statistical Analysis: Generated realistic parameters and evaluated performance over 100 trials.
  • Problem-Solving: Addressed challenges in sparse attack detection within large-scale networks.

Takeaways

  • Effectiveness of Lasso Regression: Highly effective in detecting localized attacks in large-scale networks.
  • Realistic Noise Modeling: Demonstrated the importance of Gaussian and adversarial noise simulation for realistic testing.
  • Practical Applications: Showcased adaptability to systems like power grids and telecommunications networks.
  • Integration of Theory and Practice: Highlighted the practical application of mathematical optimization to real-world challenges.
